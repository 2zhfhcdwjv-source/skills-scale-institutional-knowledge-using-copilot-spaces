# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

OctoAcme uses a structured, end-to-end project management lifecycle designed to turn ideas into measurable outcomes through clear phases, ownership, and continuous learning. Work begins in initiation, where teams define the problem, objective, success metrics, stakeholders, rough timeline, initial risks, and required roles. A lightweight one-pager acts as the core alignment artifact, and a decision gate ensures projects only move forward when goals are clear, stakeholder support is in place, and capacity is available.

In the planning phase, OctoAcme translates approved initiatives into actionable delivery plans. Teams run kickoff sessions, build and prioritize a backlog with explicit acceptance criteria, estimate effort, define Definition of Done, and map dependencies, milestones, and release timing. Risk and dependency management is embedded early through a maintained risk register and regular cross-team visibility, helping reduce surprises during execution. This phase emphasizes realistic capacity planning and clear ownership to support predictable delivery.

During execution and tracking, OctoAcme relies on a consistent team rhythm and transparent workflow management. Teams use project boards with defined status columns, run standups and delivery syncs, and review progress in sprint or milestone demos. Quality is enforced through testing layers (unit, integration, smoke tests), CI checks, security scanning, and PR standards such as linking issues, documenting acceptance criteria, and requiring reviews. Progress is monitored with operational and delivery metrics, while blockers follow a defined escalation path from team triage to sponsor-level attention when business impact is significant.

Finally, OctoAcme treats release, communication, and improvement as core operational disciplines. Releases require readiness checks (CI, security, notes, rollback planning), deployment verification, and stakeholder communication. Risks and status updates are communicated through repeatable templates and a single source of truth, ensuring stakeholders stay aligned. After sprints, milestones, and incidents, retrospectives capture what worked, what didn't, and which actions to take next; these improvements are tracked with owners and due dates, reinforcing a culture of iterative process refinement and organizational learning.
